### 1.4 Methodology Notes

- **Non-missing definition**: Finite values that do not match NetCDF fill values (`-9999.0`, `-9999`, `-127`, or variable-specific `_FillValue`/`missing_value` attributes).
- **Default statistics**: Computed on all non-missing values regardless of QC flag. The `--good-only` flag restricts to records where the corresponding variable flag (`Q_flag`, `SSC_flag`, `SSL_flag`) equals 0.
- **Log10 statistics**: Computed only on positive (>0) values. SSC and SSL are always log-transformed; Q is plotted on a linear scale by default (log10 scale available via `--q-log-scale`).
- **Spatial units**: For the master file (cluster-based), the spatial unit ID is the `cluster_id`. For the climatology file (station-based), it is the `station_uid`. Thus the cluster counts below represent basin-based clusters for the main dataset and individual stations for climatology.

## 4. Detailed Resolution Breakdown

### 4.1 Daily Resolution (2,816,013 records, 1,479 clusters)

The daily resolution constitutes the vast majority of the dataset (95.78% of records):

- **Q and SSL** are present in >99.4% of daily records across 1,456 clusters.
- **SSC** is present in 96.61% of daily records, but spanning all 1,479 clusters (100% cluster coverage).
- **Q + SSC + SSL complete triplets**: 2,714,633 records (96.40%) — the core daily coincident dataset.

### 4.2 Monthly Resolution (121,403 records, 1,863 clusters)

Monthly aggregates show the highest consistency:

- All three variables achieve near 100% record coverage (99.86–100%).
- All 1,863 clusters have complete Q + SSC + SSL data (99.86% triplets).
- This resolution adds 1,863 clusters not available at daily resolution, significantly expanding spatial coverage.

### 4.3 Annual Resolution (619 records, 58 clusters)

The smallest resolution class:

- SSC is available for all 619 records across all 58 clusters.
- Q and SSL are available for 499 records (80.61%) across 34 clusters (58.62%).
- The discrepancy reflects SSC-only stations that lack concurrent Q measurements at annual scale.

### 4.4 Climatology (1,359 records, 1,359 stations)

Climatology data (long-term means) are individual stations, not basin clusters:

- SSL has the highest availability at 98.23% (1,335 stations).
- Q and SSC are present at ~60% of stations (819 and 841 respectively).
- Only 817 stations (60.12%) have complete Q + SSC + SSL climatological data.
